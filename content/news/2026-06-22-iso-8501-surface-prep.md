# ISO 8501 Surface Preparation Grades

Choosing between Sa 1, Sa 2, Sa 2, and Sa 3 isn't an academic exercise. Each grade directly affects:

- **Adhesion strength** — typically 30–60% difference between adjacent grades
- **Service life** — each step up roughly doubles expected coating lifetime
- **Project cost** — blast to Sa 3 costs ~3x Sa 2

## The Four Grades at a Glance

| Grade | Description | Typical Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Sa 1 | Light blast, ~70% clean | Temporary protection, low-spec structures |
| Sa 2 | Thorough blast, ~95% clean | Standard industrial projects |
| Sa 2.5 | Very thorough blast, ~99% clean | ISO 12944 C4–C5 environments |
| Sa 3 | White metal, 100% clean | Immersion service, food contact |

## How to Specify

Most industrial B2B projects should specify **Sa 2.5 minimum**. Anything less means you're accepting shorter service life.

For coastal / offshore / chemical plants, push for **Sa 3 with profile 75–100 μm** to anchor thick film systems.

## Reference Standards

- ISO 8501-1 — Visual assessment
- ISO 8503 — Surface roughness
- ISO 12944 — Corrosion protection
